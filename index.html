A tree is a tall plant that can live for a very long time. It has a single stem or trunk and branches that support leaves. Beneath the ground, a tree has a root system that acts as an anchor and stores the water and nutrients the plant needs to grow.

Trees reduce the 'Greenhouse' effect by removing carbon dioxide from the air and releasing oxygen. Each year a mature tree produces enough oxygen for 10 people. Trees are also an effective sound barrier and can limit noise pollution. Recent research shows that trees also help reduce the stress of modern life.

Forests occupy 30% of the Earth's total area and are home to over a billion trees, and the current world's tree population is estimated at more than 3 trillion. What is this? A recent Yale University study found that for every person on the planet, there are around 422 trees.
